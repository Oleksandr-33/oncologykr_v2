# STRATEGY.md - Development Strategy for Oncologykr

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the comprehensive development strategy to transform oncologykr into Ukraine's leading oncology information platform. The strategy focuses on becoming a trusted resource for patients, families, and healthcare providers seeking oncology information, services, and support.

---

## Vision Statement

**"To be Ukraine's most trusted, accessible, and comprehensive digital platform for oncology care - empowering patients with knowledge, connecting them with specialists, and supporting families through every step of the cancer journey."**

---

## Mission Statement

We commit to:
1. **Inform** - Provide accurate, verified medical information in accessible Ukrainian language
2. **Connect** - Bridge the gap between patients and oncology specialists
3. **Support** - Offer resources and guidance for patients and caregivers
4. **Innovate** - Continuously improve digital healthcare accessibility
5. **Comply** - Maintain highest standards of medical accuracy and data privacy

---

## Target Audience Analysis

### Primary Audiences

#### 1. Patients Diagnosed with Cancer
**Demographics:**
- Age: 40-75 years (primary), 25-40 years (secondary)
- Location: Kryvyi Rih and Dnipropetrovsk Oblast region
- Digital literacy: Low to moderate
- Primary device: Mobile phone (65%), Desktop (35%)

**Needs:**
- Understanding their diagnosis
- Learning about treatment options
- Finding qualified specialists
- Booking appointments
- Accessing test results
- Managing treatment schedules

**Pain Points:**
- Medical jargon confusion
- Uncertainty about treatment paths
- Long wait times for information
- Difficulty navigating healthcare system
- Emotional distress and anxiety

#### 2. Family Members & Caregivers
**Demographics:**
- Age: 30-55 years
- Relationship: Spouse, adult children, siblings
- Digital literacy: Moderate to high
- Primary concern: Supporting loved ones

**Needs:**
- Understanding loved one's condition
- Learning how to provide support
- Finding support groups
- Managing logistics (appointments, medications)
- Accessing resources for caregivers

**Pain Points:**
- Feeling helpless
- Information overload
- Burnout and stress
- Financial concerns
- Work-life balance during caregiving

#### 3. Referring Physicians & Healthcare Providers
**Demographics:**
- General practitioners in the region
- Specialists seeking second opinions
- Medical professionals for referrals

**Needs:**
- Quick access to specialist directories
- Understanding available services
- Referral procedures
- Contact information for consultations

### Secondary Audiences

#### 4. Medical Students & Residents
- Learning about oncology practice in Ukraine
- Understanding clinical pathways
- Research opportunities

#### 5. Health Officials & Administrators
- Service capacity information
- Compliance documentation
- Quality metrics

## Competitive Analysis Approach

### Direct Competitors

1. **National Cancer Institute of Ukraine (cancer.gov.ua)**
   - Strengths: Official government authority, comprehensive research
   - Weaknesses: Less patient-focused, complex navigation
   - Opportunity: More accessible, patient-friendly content

2. **Private Oncology Clinics (Oberig, Dobrobut)**
   - Strengths: Modern websites, English options
   - Weaknesses: Paid services only, Kyiv-focused
   - Opportunity: Free public service, local focus

3. **Medical Information Portals (likar.info, doc.ua)**
   - Strengths: Broad medical coverage, appointment booking
   - Weaknesses: Not oncology-specialized, advertising-heavy
   - Opportunity: Specialized expertise, ad-free experience

### Indirect Competitors

1. **Facebook Groups** - Patient communities, unverified info
2. **YouTube Channels** - Educational content, varying quality
3. **WhatsApp/Telegram Groups** - Peer support, informal

### Competitive Advantages to Build

1. **Specialized Focus** - Only oncology, not general medicine

4. **Government Partnership** - NSZU compliance and official standing

6. **Mobile Excellence** - PWA with offline capability

## Brand Positioning

### Brand Essence
**"Guidance through uncertainty"**

### Brand Personality
- **Trustworthy** - Verified, official, reliable
- **Compassionate** - Understanding, supportive, human
- **Clear** - Simple, accessible, jargon-free
- **Modern** - Contemporary, tech-forward, efficient
- **Local** - Regional pride, community connection

### Brand Voice Guidelines

**Tone:**
- Warm but professional
- Reassuring but honest
- Simple but not condescending
- Hopeful but realistic

**Language:**
- Use Ukrainian as primary language
- Explain medical terms when used
- Write at 8th-grade reading level
- Use active voice
- Keep sentences short (15-20 words max)

**Examples:**

| Instead of... | Write... |
|---------------|----------|
| "Neoplasm staging requires histopathological verification" | "Determining your cancer stage requires a tissue sample test" |
| "Contact the dispensary" | "Call us at..." |
| "Treatment modalities" | "Treatment options" |
| "Submit documentation" | "Bring these documents" |

### Visual Identity Principles

**Colors:**
- Primary: Teal (#0b5f66) - Trust, healing, medical
- Secondary: Soft coral - Warmth, hope
- Neutrals: Blue-graphite for dark theme

**Typography:**
- Clean, readable sans-serif
- Larger text for accessibility
- High contrast ratios

**Imagery:**
- Real people, not stock photos
- Diverse age representation
- Hopeful, not clinical
- Ukrainian context

### Brand Promise
*"We promise to provide accurate, accessible oncology information when you need it most, connecting you with the specialists and resources to navigate your cancer journey with confidence."*

## Strategic Priorities

### Priority 1: Trust & Credibility
- Ensure all medical content is verified by licensed oncologists
- Display credentials and certifications prominently
- Cite sources for medical information
- Partner with medical institutions for endorsement

### Priority 2: Accessibility
- Maintain WCAG 2.1 AA compliance
- Optimize for low-bandwidth connections
- Support older devices and browsers
- Provide content in multiple formats (text, audio, video)

### Priority 3: Patient Empowerment
- Make complex information understandable
- Provide clear next steps and calls-to-action
- Enable self-service where appropriate
- Support informed decision-making

### Priority 4: Operational Efficiency
- Reduce phone inquiries through comprehensive FAQ
- Enable online appointment requests
- Provide downloadable forms and documents
- Automate common workflows

### Priority 5: Continuous Improvement
- Collect user feedback systematically
- Monitor analytics for insights
- Update content based on policy changes
- Iterate based on user needs

## Risk Assessment

### High-Impact Risks

| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|------------|
| Medical misinformation | Critical | Medium | Mandatory medical review for all content |
| Data breach | Critical | Low | GDPR compliance, minimal data collection |
| Website downtime | High | Low | Static hosting, CDN, monitoring |
| Outdated information | High | Medium | Content calendar, update triggers |

### Medium-Impact Risks

| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|------------|
| Low user adoption | Medium | Medium | Marketing, SEO, referral partnerships |
| Accessibility gaps | Medium | Medium | Regular audits, user testing |
| Mobile performance | Medium | Low | PWA optimization, testing |
| Staff resistance | Medium | Medium | Training, demonstrated value |

## Governance Structure

### Content Governance
- **Medical Director** - Final approval for clinical content
- **Content Manager** - Day-to-day content updates
- **Technical Lead** - Website functionality and performance
- **Quality Reviewer** - Accuracy and compliance checks

### Review Cycles
- **Clinical content**: Annual review + update on policy changes
- **Service information**: Quarterly review
- **Doctor directory**: Monthly verification
- **Technical audit**: Bi-annual

### Approval Workflow
1. Content drafted by content manager
2. Medical review by licensed oncologist
3. Technical review for accessibility
4. Final approval by medical director
5. Publication and indexing
